LENS

Brief

Design : 2017 October - 2017 December
Role : Graphic Designer, UI/UX Designer
Team Members : Harry Wong, Wendy Lu, Shirley Mao
Tools : Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Objective

In a speculative design course, a project required teams to design a speculative object for a provocation. Speculative means designing for the future, which tested students the understanding, interpreting, and thinking of future designs.

Response

We decided to create a privacy device for parents. This device was attached to the back of a child’s neck, where it had the ability to track the child’s biometrics. A phone application was used to receive the data and display ir for parents to see.

SUMMARY

This broad topic allowed us to focus on areas that we were interested in. As a group of three, we decided to create a privacy device for parents. This device was attached to the back of a child’s neck, where it had the ability to track the child’s biometrics. A phone application was used to receive the data and display for the parents to see. This project commented on how much privacy a child can get with their parents before it becomes too strict or lenient.

PROCESS

The process begins with all three of us pitching an idea to one another. I pitched the idea of focusing on biometric data and how it can be hacked. Many critiques and feedback later, we combined biometric data and children mobile use together. This left us with the core idea that we see in our final project.

MY ROLE

Since my strengths revolved around visuals, I was in charge of all graphic design work, and wireframing of the mobile application. In the beginning stages of designing posters and wireframes, I had created several different versions so that my team can choose which designs were working and which ones were not. Many elements came into consideration, such as where to place images and icons, what font type and font size to use, and what the colour palette was going to be. In addition to wireframing the screens, I also determined the workflow of the app. The workflow was placed into a flowchart like template that showed the user which screen led to which screens. This user interface workflow became vital, as it became the guide for the animated user interface video.

FINAL PRODUCT

In the final showcase, we had shown six posters in total, a video scenario, and an animated user interface video. Our project was one of the strongest in the entire class from the beginning to end. This is in large part due to the fact that every team member overachieved in their assigned work, and gave major critiques to one another.
